# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mobile-first MiniPay and Celo users who want fast, social skill games with stablecoin stakes. They may be casual players, crypto-curious users, or friends joining a private room by code. They need the app to feel simple, trustworthy, and quick on small screens.

## Product Purpose

Sensa is a color memory game hub on Celo. It lets players practice for free, play solo against memory, create private rooms, join multiplayer matches, stake USDC, resolve results on-chain, and withdraw winnings from a vault.

Success means users understand the game loop without crypto explanations, trust where their stake goes, and can complete play, result, vault, and withdraw flows on mobile without friction.

## Brand Personality

Playful, punchy, trustworthy.

The voice can be cheeky and game-like, but money actions must stay clear and calm. Gameplay should feel tactile and competitive; wallet and vault flows should feel safe, direct, and MiniPay-friendly.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic DeFi dashboards: token tables, yield language, protocol jargon, and wallet-first framing.

Avoid casino UI: flashing rewards, fake jackpot hype, manipulative urgency, and cluttered badges.

Avoid generic purple SaaS polish. Purple is already part of the app identity, so future work should use it deliberately with stronger layout, better rhythm, and clearer product hierarchy rather than adding more purple decoration.

## Design Principles

Mobile first, MiniPay first. The app must work at 360px width with thumb-friendly controls and wallet copy that matches MiniPay expectations.

Game first, chain second. Users should feel they are playing a skill game, not operating a protocol.

Trust is a visible state. Stake, refund, result, vault, and withdraw states must explain what happened without raw blockchain noise.

One action per moment. Each screen should make the next move obvious, especially during room creation, staking, submitting, resolving, and withdrawing.

Use playfulness as feedback, not clutter. Motion, icons, color, and copy should reward progress without hiding the core decision.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target practical WCAG AA for product-critical text, buttons, forms, and wallet states. Preserve reduced-motion alternatives for animated gameplay and transitions.

Use MiniPay-safe language in user-facing copy: Stablecoin, Deposit, Withdraw, Network fee. Avoid Gas, crypto, onramp, and offramp in UI copy.

Never rely on raw wallet addresses as the primary user-facing identity. Prefer nicknames, short labels, or generated player names when possible.
